How to assess requirements [My way] -- Part 1

2016-12-22

I have been an analyst for almost 8 project within my first 2 years working as Business Analyst. Harsh truth, especially you need to do your job with minimum supervising, which left you alone to figure out how to make things work.

And analyst is a front liner. They do things wrong, everything goes wrong from cost estimation to project timeline and from developer tasks to final product. So please be real cautious when you’re doing your job as analyst, especially for projects.

After those projects, I found myself repeating same pattern over and over again. These pattern might be already described at several book or article of Requirement Engineering, but to be honest I don’t really pay attention to those book. I did read some of them, but there are some condition in real working world that just doesn’t work the same as those book, where you need to improvise all you can to ensure the safety of the project.

So, here’s my how-to:

How-to assess requirements

Read the Business Requirements … A lot!

One thing that somehow missed by lot of people when building an application is, what this application will solve? What the use of features at this application. Understanding a Business Requirement is so important that you need to really understand what they need, NOT WHAT THEY WANT.

Client tend to make requests, but always tight on budget, both money and time. So you need to make sure you will deliver thing they need. Because what they want somehow always need a more amount of money then they are willing to pay.

Understanding business requirements of the application will also help you adjust the implementation of business process into your application. You can trim several process that is fancy but not necessary. Use ‘Use Case Method’ to simulate and analyse the business requirements. You’ll be happy.

And it helps you negotiate with client. Remember that you need client approval to proceed, so if you really understand what kind of business they need you to solve, you can move into next part.

Drive them into right solution

Most of the time, PM and BA consider saying ‘yes’ to each request asked by client. Sometimes you need to do that because those are exactly what client need, but lot of other time, you need to make a clear line that those feature they were asking will not make the application any better, instead of raising the cost they need to pay.

I don’t hate fancy stuff, but I hate unnecessary stuff. If they need dinner than dinner it is, not a wedding cake. They’re different in usage, even tough they’re food all the same. Some fancy might be needed depends on the business requirement you try to solve. For example, they want all data create a history log when changed. With this, you will add a lot of work to developer, maintaining status, work with all field that call these data as lookup item, etc. But actually they don’t even need a history of changes because they business process don’t even mentioned the need of historical data. And almost no data will be updated since they all came from locked data. Depending on your understanding to business process and requirements, you will agree or object this request.

So right after they finish talking about how their business works, take a deep breath, analyse them, and start steering your client into the planned solution direction. Don’t cut everything but don’t allow everything. Keep it balanced, you really need to understand thing they need and things the simply want.

Keep communication until they sign

Don’t guess anything. This is their money, they have the right to know what they will get. So whenever you feel confused about one or two requirements, keep asking and confirming your client till they answer. Always throw the ball at them whenever you find difficulties.

Discussion with your developer

This is important part. Sometimes our ego and feel of experienced is too high, we forgot to ask those who will work on this application. Especially if you have works on lot of project and has technical background in software industry. But it is worthy to ask your developer, so you can communicate client with more precise answer instead of guess.

Developer team can help you find out how to implement the application. Software architect can help you find out what architecture is best for the solution. Discussion with developer team can becomes a powerful evidence to talk with client, instead of blabbering things about business process – which they own. Not a great plan.

Next time, I will talk more about practical approach of requirement assessment, all things start with abstract and mentality. So first, you need to understand what they need and want, how they want it, steer them into your pace, and then Discuss with developer and keep communication to both parties, you can find it easier to translate the business requirements into software requirements.
